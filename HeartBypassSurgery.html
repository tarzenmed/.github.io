Heart Bypass Surgery Cabg

  1. Home
  2. Heart Bypass Surgery Cabg

Coronary Artery Bypass Graft (CABG) Surgery, commonly referred to as Heart Bypass Surgery, is a critical and highly effective surgical intervention used to address significant blockages in the coronary arteries. These arteries supply vital oxygen and nutrients to the heart muscle. During CABG, skilled cardiac surgeons work to reestablish unimpeded blood flow to the heart by bypassing narrowed or obstructed segments of coronary arteries. This is achieved by utilizing blood vessels, typically taken from the patient's own body, such as the internal mammary artery or veins from the leg.



These harvested vessels are then skillfully grafted onto the affected areas, creating an alternative route for blood to flow around the blockages. This bypass pathway effectively circumvents the narrowed segments, ensuring an ample supply of oxygenated blood to the heart muscle. CABG is a meticulously performed procedure that addresses severe cases of coronary artery disease, a condition characterized by the buildup of fatty deposits (plaque) within the arteries. This can lead to reduced blood flow, chest pain (angina), and even heart attacks. By restoring proper blood circulation to the heart, CABG helps alleviate symptoms, enhance cardiac function, and significantly improve the patient's quality of life. This procedure stands as a cornerstone in cardiac surgery, offering renewed hope and vitality to individuals facing serious coronary artery issues.

How is the Procedure Performed?

The CABG procedure is a meticulously orchestrated surgical intervention to address significant blockages in the coronary arteries. It begins with a precise incision along the chest, allowing access to the heart and its blood vessels.
A healthy artery or vein, often sourced from the patient's own body (commonly the internal mammary artery or leg veins), is carefully harvested. This vessel is then skillfully grafted onto the affected area of the coronary artery. The grafted vessel serves as a detour, creating a new pathway for oxygen-rich blood to navigate around the blockage.
By establishing this alternative route, the bypassed blood flow circumvents the narrowed or obstructed segment, ensuring an ample supply of oxygenated blood to the heart muscle. This effectively restores normal blood flow, alleviating symptoms of angina and reducing the risk of heart attacks.

The CABG procedure is a remarkable feat of surgical expertise, offering renewed vitality and improved cardiac function to individuals with severe coronary artery disease. It stands as a cornerstone in cardiac surgery, exemplifying the power of modern medical interventions in preserving and enhancing heart health.

Who is the Right Doctor for Heart Bypass Surgery Cabg?

For Coronary Artery Bypass Surgery (CABG), the most suitable specialist to consult is a Cardiac Surgeon. These highly skilled and specialized surgeons possess extensive training and expertise in performing intricate heart surgeries, including CABG. They have a deep understanding of the complexities of the cardiovascular system and are equipped to handle the technical demands of bypass procedures. Cardiac Surgeons work closely with a multidisciplinary team to ensure comprehensive pre-operative assessments, precise surgical execution, and thorough post-operative care. Their proficiency in cardiac surgery makes them the optimal choice for individuals seeking effective interventions for significant coronary artery blockages.

What is the Usual Success Rate for this Procedure?

The success rate is usually high and varies between 82-94%.
Some possible complications include -

  • Bleeding
  • Blood Clot Formation
  • Infection
  • Fever
  • Stroke

What is included in the cost of Heart Bypass Surgery Cabg?

The cost of a Heart Bypass Surgery includes:
  • Preoperative diagnostic tests cost [blood tests, a chest X-ray, an echocardiogram (ECG) and an electrocardiogram (EKG)]
  • Surgery cost
  • Post-Operative cost (depends on the number of follow-up sessions)
  • Medicine cost (anticoagulant, antihypertensive, antibiotics, etc.)
  • Patient's hospital stay
  • Note:Patients undergoing heart bypass surgery should pay special attention to their diet and avoid stress.

What are the factors affecting cost of Heart Bypass Surgery Cabg?

The overall cost of the procedure also varies based on the patient's condition and preferences. Some of these factors are:
  • Type of hospital and room opted (General, Twin sharing, or single room)
  • Post-surgical complication, if it happens (such as wound infection, the prolonged requirement for mechanical ventilation, etc.)
  • Cost of blood products (if required)
  • The severity of the disease
  • Cost of accommodation during follow-ups, in case the patient is not a local resident
